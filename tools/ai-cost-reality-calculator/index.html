The measurement rule

A token is a meter reading. It is not a business result.

This tool calculates the fully loaded cost of building, running, reviewing, correcting, governing, and maintaining an AI use case—then divides that cost by outcomes the business actually verified.

Methodology

Begin with the business result, then count what makes it usable.

Most AI calculators begin with model cost and end with an impressive ROI percentage. This one begins with the business result that must actually be delivered—and then counts everything required to make that result accurate, usable, governed, and complete.

Learn how the calculation was built

Most AI cost discussions begin with tokens, API calls, or hours saved. Those numbers describe activity. They do not prove that the business received a usable result.

This framework starts somewhere else:

What did the organization actually receive, and what did it cost to make that outcome acceptable?
1. Why model cost is not the full cost

An AI output may still require human review, correction, policy and compliance approval, exception handling, system integration, monitoring, maintenance, escalation, and downstream remediation.

The model invoice is one part of the cost stack—not the entire business case.

2. Why the calculator uses verified outcomes
Attempt
The workflow starts or the system produces an output.
Completed output
The technical workflow reaches its end.
First-pass verified outcome
The result passes without material correction.
Reviewed-pass outcome
A human reviews and approves it without correction; this is not autonomous.
Corrected verified outcome
The result becomes acceptable after material correction.
Failure
The attempt is rejected, abandoned, reopened, reversed, or causes a defined exception.

A prompt is not a result. An API call is not a result. A technically completed workflow is not necessarily a verified business outcome.

3. The primary calculation
Fully Loaded AI Cost per Verified Outcome = Unit-Economics AI TCO ÷ Verified Outcomes
Unit-Economics AI TCO = Direct Model and Platform Cost + Human Review Cost + Rework and Exception Cost + Escalation Cost + Other Recurring AI Operating Cost + Annualized Implementation Cost + Annualized Refresh Cost

Cost per attempt and cost per verified outcome appear side by side because the difference reveals the correction and failure burden.

4. Why capacity is not treated as cash

Saved time becomes hard financial value only when there is evidence of payroll, contractor cost, or overtime removed; approved hiring avoided; retired software or process cost; or measurable incremental contribution.

Released capacity may be valuable. It is not automatically cash savings.

Finance does not deposit hours. — Sudeep Arya
5. Why the calculator shows three TCO views

Year 1 cash TCO answers how much cash the initiative requires in its first year. Steady-state annual TCO shows the ongoing run rate. Unit-economics TCO adds annualized implementation and refresh so cost can be compared with verified outcomes.

6. What is original about the framework

The AI Cost Reality Framework is an original decision framework developed by Sudeep Arya. It applies established finance, FinOps, risk-management, and unit-economics methods to measuring fully loaded AI cost per verified business outcome.

Established concepts include TCO, ROI, NPV, payback, expected loss, break-even, contribution margin, and unit economics. The original contribution is the combined system: verified outcomes as the controlling denominator; cost per attempt beside cost per verified outcome; human correction inside cost; capacity separate from cash; three TCO views; evidence labels; a governance gate; ecommerce outcome templates; and integrated cost, quality, benefit, and risk engines.

7. What the calculator can and cannot tell you

It can help assess verified unit cost, hard financial value, payback, risk-adjusted economics, evidence coverage, and whether more production evidence is required.

It cannot decide whether people should be eliminated, reach legal or employment conclusions, determine accounting treatment, provide investment advice, or make weak assumptions valid because they were entered into a tool.

The purpose of the calculator is not to make every AI initiative look attractive. Its purpose is to make the economics visible.

Decision support only. This tool is not accounting, legal, investment, employment, cybersecurity, privacy, or regulatory advice. It does not recommend eliminating jobs.

Method

Fully loaded cost per verified outcome

The calculator combines standard finance mathematics, FinOps unit economics, and an author-designed verified-outcome framework. It keeps Year 1 cash TCO, steady-state TCO, and annualized unit-economics TCO separate.

TCO
Total cost of ownership: implementation, model/platform, people, integration, governance, review, rework, and operations.
Verified outcome
A result that meets the defined quality, policy, compliance, and completion standard.
Hard benefit
Realized cash, contribution, approved avoidance, or measured loss avoided—not a blanket value for time saved.
Capacity value
A directional value for released hours, displayed separately from hard cash benefit.
Risk-adjusted ROI
An author-designed decision metric that subtracts expected annual loss and includes it in the denominator.
