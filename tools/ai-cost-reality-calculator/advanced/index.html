05 Assumptions and completeness

    Methodology and limits

    Detailed where useful. Comparable throughout.

    Advanced accepts known totals or builds them from direct components. Inactive methods never enter the calculation.

    Current unit cost×AI attempts × accepted rate= Maximum AI cost at break-even
    Cost methods and double-counting protection

    Known-total and component-builder methods are mutually exclusive. Current labor builders derive direct process cost. AI builders add technology, allocated implementation, review, and other operations exactly once.

    Implementation and review calculations

    One-time implementation cost is divided by 1–10 amortization years, then allocated to the selected period. Review cost can be entered directly or calculated from attempts × review rate × review minutes ÷ 60 × loaded hourly cost.

    Equivalent output and thresholds

    Equivalent-output cost compares both processes at the current accepted-output volume. The maximum break-even AI cost shows how much total AI cost the entered accepted rate can support.

    Limitations

    The model does not automatically account for demand shifts, nonlinear scale, vendor tiers, tax, financing, working capital, discount rates, legal exposure, implementation delays, ramp time, unentered change-management cost, revenue effects, opportunity cost, inflation, or model-quality drift. It is not financial advice, an audit, a guaranteed ROI model, or a replacement for finance, legal, or compliance review.

    Privacy

    Calculations run entirely in this browser. The page uses no calculator analytics, form submission, local storage, cookies, external calculation service, or remote logging.
