      Methodology 1.0

      Useful output, defined before calculation.

      All calculations run in the browser. Inputs and results are never sent, stored, or placed in the URL.

      Read the operating guide

      What content operations means

      Content operations is the connected system of truth, assets, requests, workflow, ownership, review, approval, channel execution, measurement, and learning that turns a request into authorized content. AI-assisted productivity is useful only when the system can distinguish attempts from approved output and planned review from correction.

      Attempted versus approved output

      An attempted deliverable enters creation whether it is approved, corrected, abandoned, failed, or unresolved. An approved deliverable has completed the required review process and is authorized for its intended channel and use. Approval yield is finally approved output divided by attempted output. First-pass approval is the portion authorized without a correction cycle.

      Standard review and rework burden

      Standard review is the mandatory review planned for every attempted deliverable. It is not rework. Correction/rework is additional effort used to repair an output. Rework burden divides correction hours by total modeled labor hours. Quick Mode combines review and correction and cannot diagnose them separately.

      Cost per approved deliverable

      Full Workflow Analysis divides modeled attributable workflow cost by approved output. The model includes entered labor and only those technology, external, and implementation categories explicitly marked included. Known zero remains distinct from excluded or not estimated.

      Formula registry 1.0.0

      Attempts = requests × attempted deliverables per request

      Approved = attempts × final approval rate

      Total labor = creation + standard review + correction + exception + governance

      Modeled attributable workflow cost = labor + included technology + included external + amortized implementation

      Cost per approved = modeled attributable workflow cost ÷ approved

      Approved capacity = [(available hours − exception − governance) ÷ variable labor per attempt] × approval yield

      PIM readiness, DAM readiness, and keeping the stack simple

      Evaluate PIM capability when repeated channel work suffers from conflicting product facts, claims, qualifiers, or variations. Evaluate DAM capability when asset versions, rights, metadata, and distribution create recurring complexity. A maintained table or shared library may remain appropriate when ownership is clear and change is limited. Build, buy, or keep simple is an operating decision, not a maturity badge.

      Potential capacity versus cash savings

      Potential capacity released is modeled time available for reassignment at equivalent approved output. It is not realized cash savings, does not imply that a cost disappears, and must never be treated as a headcount recommendation.

      Hypothetical worked example

      A growing brand receives 40 requests per month, attempts two deliverables per request, spends 1.2 hours creating and 0.5 hours reviewing or correcting each attempt, uses a $60 loaded hourly rate, and approves 75% of attempts. The model returns 80 attempts, 60 approved outputs, 136 labor hours, 29.4% review/correction burden, $8,160 modeled labor cost, and $136 labor cost per approved deliverable. This is an illustration, not an industry benchmark.

      KPI glossary

      Approval yield
      Finally approved deliverables divided by attempts.
      First-pass approval
      Attempts approved without a correction cycle.
      Review burden
      Standard review hours divided by total modeled labor.
      Rework burden
      Correction hours divided by total modeled labor.
      Modeled attributable workflow cost
      Entered workflow labor plus included cost categories.
      Most influential entered assumption
      The valid proposed input whose plus or minus 10% perturbation creates the largest absolute percentage movement in cost per approved deliverable. It is not a prediction or causal claim.

      Readiness model

      The assessment covers product truth, brand truth, asset control, workflow, ownership, exceptions, approved AI inputs, human approval, measurement, and learning. Raw stages are constrained by critical gates. The result is an original sequencing and decision aid, not an industry benchmark, certification, percentile, or independently validated maturity model.

      Limitations and privacy

      The planner does not forecast revenue, cash savings, headcount requirements, market performance, or technology ROI. It does not transmit company names, assessment answers, stages, workflow values, labor or cost data, KPIs, CSV content, clipboard content, or free text. It uses no analytics, cookies, storage, Netlify Forms, external AI service, or personalized result URL.

      A future dedicated local-only “Export Results as PDF” may use locally hosted, version-pinned browser code and a shared export standard with the AI Cost Reality Calculator. This release uses browser Print / Save as PDF and does not add a PDF library.

      Frequently asked questions

      Do I need to complete readiness before calculating?

      No. All three paths are independent.

      Why is approved output the denominator?

      Attempts include failed, abandoned, corrected, and unresolved work. Approved output reflects what the team authorizes for intended use.

      Is a higher readiness stage always better?

      No. Stop at the stage that solves the operating problem with adequate control.

      Does potential capacity equal savings?

      No. It is modeled time available for reassignment, not a realized financial result.

      Are recommendations generated by AI?

      No. They are deterministic rules tied to the entered controls and economics.
