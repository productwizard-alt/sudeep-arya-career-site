# Executive redesign v4 — internal audit

## Retain

- Approved claims, role history, case-study anchors, schema, analytics, sitemap, redirects, forms, Calendly routes, downloads, and print support.
- The AI Cost Reality Calculator five-input flow, Formula 1.1.0, defaults, privacy model, tooltips, result logic, and tests.
- Running Before Crawling citations, corrected claims, graphics, PDF, and calculator bridge.
- Existing profile photography and publication cover as secondary editorial assets.

## Remove or reduce

- Homepage Commerce Scope dashboard, nested identity card, six-card libraries, repeated capability copy, oversized paper feature, and competing hero actions.
- Oversized image heroes, repeated dark panels, large fixed/minimum heights, excessive section padding, rounded cards, shadow-heavy panels, and decorative route-map complexity.
- Duplicate homepage résumé timeline and secondary proof that belongs on the résumé or case-study routes.

## Rebuild

- Header as a compact 68px editorial bar, compressing to 56px after scroll; Contact is the sole outlined action while Audit returns to the normal hierarchy.
- Homepage as six content-driven movements: executive hero, proof ledger, research/tool bridge, three featured cases, capability disclosures, and one closing invitation.
- Shared type, spacing, divider, button, focus, radius, shadow, width, and motion tokens across all routes.
- Interior heroes to a content-led 250–360px range, with more compact mobile spacing.
- Footer as a legible brand/navigation close rather than a large interactive destination map.

## Progressive disclosure

- Preserve and refine case-study detail drawers and direct anchors.
- Use native details/summary for the homepage capability system, giving desktop a two-column editorial layout and mobile a compact accordion.
- Keep primary outcomes and role facts visible; reserve methodology and supporting platform detail for disclosure.

## Hierarchy and responsive findings

- The former homepage required excessive scrolling because proof, career history, capabilities, case studies, audit, paper, and speaking each occupied separate tall sections.
- Primary proof appeared too late and was visually diluted by repeated cards.
- Mobile inherited desktop module density and oversized hero behavior; the redesign changes grids, portrait treatment, ledgers, case rows, capability navigation, and calls to action rather than merely stacking them.
- The new direction uses warm editorial paper, deep navy anchors, teal as a single action/proof accent, Georgia for display copy, and the system sans for interface/body copy.
