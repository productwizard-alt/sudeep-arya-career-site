Conversation topics

Start with the operating question.

Four connected pillars cover the channel, system, measurement, and leadership choices behind modern commerce.

01Amazon and marketplaces

Channel structure, operating discipline, and profitable growth

Amazon and marketplaces

How brands weigh control, margin, inventory, media, fulfillment, and team ownership across 1P, 3P, and broader marketplace models.

Sample conversation titles

  • Amazon 1P and 3P: the operating tradeoffs
  • Marketplace growth without operational disorder
  • Profitability beyond topline marketplace growth

Why it mattersChannel choices create second-order effects across pricing, content, supply, media, and accountability.

02DTC and commerce systems

Platforms, retention, and the operating model behind the storefront

DTC and commerce systems

What changes when Shopify+, subscription infrastructure, merchandising, analytics, and customer workflows must operate as one system.

Sample conversation titles

  • Shopify+ beyond replatforming
  • Subscription commerce and retention infrastructure
  • When platform work becomes operating-model work
  • The PDP is still the storefront

Why it mattersTechnology creates value only when the surrounding ownership, data, and daily workflows improve with it.

03Retail media and measurement

Media signals connected to commercial decisions

Retail media and measurement

How Sponsored Ads, DSP, AMC-style audience logic, inventory, margin, and executive reporting can support a more complete decision view.

Sample conversation titles

  • Retail media beyond ROAS
  • AMC, DSP, and audience measurement
  • Connecting media signals to commerce decisions
  • What executives actually need from commerce analytics

Why it mattersMedia metrics become more useful when leaders can connect them to customers, inventory, margin, and action.

04AI, content operations, and leadership

Workflow economics, human judgment, and change that can hold up in practice

AI, content operations, and leadership

How leaders can evaluate AI-assisted workflows through accepted output, human approval boundaries, evidence, and operating readiness.

Sample conversation titles

  • Small Team, Bigger Output
  • Cost per approved output
  • Human approval boundaries in AI-assisted work
  • Why executive AI programs stall in ordinary workflows

Why it mattersAI programs stall when governance, workflow design, content systems, and adoption are treated as afterthoughts.
